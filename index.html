Technology: Electro-Quasistatic Human Body Communication (EQS-HBC)

Our groundbreaking discovery makes secure Electro-Quasistatic region usable for the first time

Communication using electromagnetic signals (e.g. Bluetooth) radiate throughout the airwaves making the physical signal available upto 10m radius. Our "body as a wire" technology uses patent pending techniques to reduce loss at Electro-Quasistatic frequencies to keep the physical signal within the human body to create a private channel.

Key Publication: D. Das, S. Maity, B. Chatterjee, and S. Sen, "Enabling Covert Body Area Network using Electro-Quasistatic Human Body Communication," in Scientific Reports (Nature)- Feb 2019

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is Wearkey's product?

    Wearkey builds wearable hardware keys for multi-factor authentication. Our “body as a wire” technology enables wearable devices to function as a secure second factor.

  • What can Wearkey do that today’s hardware keys cannot?

    Wearkey’s multi-factor hardware tokens can be built into wearable devices/accessories without the security vulnerabilities introduced by today’s wireless communication standards.

  • Why should I use Human Body Communication instead of Bluetooth Low Energy?

    Bluetooth Low Energy uses wireless communication standards that can be spoofed/snarfed from over 10 meters away. Human body communication offers a private channel that is only accessible through physical touch.

  • Is the technology using my biometrics?

    The technology uses the body as a generic wire to communicate between the wearable key and the computer. However, wearable devices are well positioned to gather biometrics for identity verification.

  • Is this technology safe?

    Human Body Communication utilizes over 1000x times lower current than government recommended current safety limits and a safety study has been performed to show no impact on the human body over time. S. Maity et al., "On the Safety of Human Body Communication," in IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering
